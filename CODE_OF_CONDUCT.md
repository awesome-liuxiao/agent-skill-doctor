# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Be respectful, specific, and curious. Critique ideas and evidence rather than people. Welcome
newcomers, explain unfamiliar context, and make space for different experience levels and
backgrounds.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, discriminatory language, sexualized content,
deliberate intimidation, publishing another person's private information, or sustained disruption
of project spaces.

## Scope

This code applies in repository issues, pull requests, Discussions, review threads, and project
events, as well as when someone is publicly representing the project.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, remove, reject, or lock content and may temporarily or permanently restrict
participation when behavior threatens a safe and productive community. Enforcement decisions will
consider context, impact, and repeated behavior rather than popularity or technical status.

For a private conduct report, use the repository's private reporting path described in
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) and begin the report title with `Code of conduct`. Include links or
screenshots when safe to do so. Do not include credentials, private customer data, or unrelated
personal information.

Good-faith reports will be handled as confidentially as practical. Retaliation against a reporter
or participant is itself a violation.
